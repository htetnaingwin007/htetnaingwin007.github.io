The modern idea of a restaurant – as well as the term itself –
appeared in Paris in the 18th century.
For centuries Paris had taverns which
served food at large common tables, but they were notoriously
crowded, noisy, not very clean, and served food of dubious quality.
In about 1765 a new kind of eating establishment, called a "Bouillon",
was opened on rue des Poulies, near the Louvre, by a man named Boulanger.
